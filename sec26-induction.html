Section 1.3 Proof by Induction

Warmup Exercise 1.3.1.

Prove by induction that, for all \(n\in\mathbb{N}\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} 1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}. \end{equation*}
Hint

Remember, it often helps to write down all three of the following statements:

\begin{align} P(1)\colon && 1 &= \frac{1(1+1)}{2} \label{p1}\tag{1.3.1}\\ P(k)\colon && 1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + k &= \frac{k(k+1)}{2} \label{p2}\tag{1.3.2}\\ P(k+1)\colon && 1+2+3+\cdots+k+(k+1) &= \frac{(k+1)(k+1+1)}{2}\label{p3}\tag{1.3.3} \end{align}

A proof by induction must

  1. Prove (1.3.1),

  2. Assume (1.3.2), and then

  3. Prove (1.3.3) by using that assumption along the way.

Solution
